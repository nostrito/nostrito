Everything you need.
Nothing you don't.

nostrito does one thing well: keeps your Nostr data local, trusted, and available.

🕸️

Web of Trust Sync

Computes your trust graph from your follow list. Only fetches events from people you actually trust — not the entire network.

📡

Local NIP-01 Relay

A full WebSocket relay on localhost. Point Damus, Amethyst, or any client at it. Your feed, served from your machine.

🌸

Blossom Media Cache

Automatically downloads and stores images, videos, and files from Blossom URLs. Your media, cached locally, verified by hash.

🎯

Human-Friendly Config

Pick relays by name — primal, damus, nos — not WebSocket URLs. TOML config, zero friction.

⚡

Background Daemon

Starts with your computer, syncs when online, sleeps when you sleep. Single Rust binary, minimal CPU and memory footprint.

🔒

Offline-First

Your data never depends on someone else's server. Relay goes down? Doesn't matter. Your events, your media — always available.

Up and running in a minute.

No terminal. No config files. Just open the app.

Paste your npub

Open nostrito and drop in your public key. It sets up everything — database, relay connections, trust graph.

Watch it sync

nostrito discovers your network, builds your Web of Trust, and starts pulling events from people you actually follow.

Use any client

Your favorite Nostr app connects to nostrito automatically. Your feed, served from your machine — fast, private, always available.

Built to be invisible.

A single Rust binary. Five async components. One SQLite database. Runs quietly in the background.

Future: Gossip Protocol

Beyond a personal relay. nostrito's roadmap includes trust-weighted P2P gossip — turning your relay into a node in a resilient network.

  • 🧅 Tor hidden service — share your relay with trusted friends via .onion address
  • 🤝 Storage pacts — bilateral agreements with WoT peers to store each other's data
  • 💓 Proof of life — detect if missing events are a discovery issue or something more serious
  • 🔄 Tiered retrieval — local → peers → gossip → relay fallback
You (nostrito) │ ├──── Alice (pact peer) │ stores your events │ you store hers │ ├──── Bob (pact peer) │ mutual backup │ gossip bridge │ └──── Public Relay fallback only used <5% of reads Your data lives in your trust network, not on someone else's server.
